Cast Iron Pan-Seared Steak

Description

Steak! It's what's for dinner!

Everyone loves a good steak. If anyone asks you to prepare there's well-done, you ask the politely, yet firmly, to leave

Ingredients

  • 2 beef top sirloin steaks
  • 2 cups orange juice
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons steak seasoning
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • sea salt to taste

Steps

  1. Gather ingredients
  2. Place steaks side by side in a large dish. Add orange juice, cider vinegar, and Worcestershire sauce. Marinate, uncovered, in the fridge for 45 minutes
  3. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees. Remove casserole dish from fridge. Cover steaks with plastic wrap and allow them to come to room temperature
  4. Place steaks on a clean work surface and generously rub with steak seasoning and black pepper
  5. Heat olive oil in a cast-iron skillet over high heat. Cook steaks in the hot skillet until lightly browned on the bottom
  6. Flip and cook until browned on the other side and red in the center. Transfer skillet with the steaks to the oven
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until steaks are firm and reddish-pink to lightly pink in the center
  8. Remove steaks from the oven; season with salt. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving
